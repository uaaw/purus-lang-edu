# Code of Conduct

[日本語版](CODE_OF_CONDUCT-ja.md)

## Our Commitment

Purus Lang Education is committed to providing a welcoming, safe, and inclusive community for everyone. We value respectful collaboration and participation in good faith, regardless of background, identity, experience, or ability.

## Expected Behavior

Community members are expected to:

- Treat others with kindness, empathy, and respect.
- Welcome different viewpoints and experiences.
- Give and receive constructive feedback thoughtfully.
- Take responsibility for mistakes and work to repair harm.
- Keep discussions focused on the project and its community.
- Respect privacy, boundaries, and requests to stop an interaction.

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behavior is not acceptable:

- Harassment, intimidation, discrimination, or threats.
- Insults, personal attacks, trolling, or deliberately disruptive conduct.
- Unwanted sexual attention or sexualized language and imagery.
- Publishing another person's private information without permission.
- Impersonation or deliberately misleading others about identity or affiliation.
- Repeatedly ignoring clearly stated boundaries.
- Any other conduct that a reasonable person would consider harmful or inappropriate in a professional community.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces, including issues, pull requests, discussions, code reviews, documentation, community channels, and events. It also applies when someone officially represents the project in public.

## Reporting

Report possible violations privately to the project maintainers using a private contact method listed on their GitHub profiles or other official project channels.

Do not include sensitive details in a public issue. If no private contact method is available, open a minimal issue asking a maintainer to provide a private reporting channel without describing the incident publicly.

Maintainers will review reports promptly and fairly, protect the privacy and safety of everyone involved, and disclose information only when necessary to investigate or resolve the matter.

## Enforcement

Depending on the severity and history of a violation, maintainers may issue a private warning, remove content, limit participation temporarily, or permanently restrict access to project spaces. Enforcement decisions will prioritize community safety, fairness, and opportunities to repair harm where appropriate.
